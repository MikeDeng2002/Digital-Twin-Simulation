This pattern indicates low consumerism and uniqueness-seeking in terms of product acquisition, but some willingness to challenge social norms in product use.

## Risk preferences  
The survey includes multiple lottery and risk preference tasks. The respondent consistently prefers the lottery option over certain smaller amounts until the certain amount approaches or exceeds the expected value, indicating moderate risk aversion. For example, she prefers a 50% chance at $6 over any certain amount less than $6, but switches preference when the certain amount reaches or exceeds $6. Similarly, for lotteries with higher stakes, she prefers the lottery until the certain amount is close to or above the expected value. For losses, she prefers certain smaller losses over lotteries with larger potential losses, indicating risk aversion in losses as well.

In the WTA/WTP dictator and ultimatum games, she proposes to keep $2 and send $3 to the other person, and as a receiver, she accepts all offers including the least favorable ($0 for self). In the trust game, she would send all $5 to the other person (maximizing the tripled amount) and as receiver, she would keep $5 and send back $10 out of $15, indicating a generous but balanced social preference.

## Intertemporal patience  
In intertemporal choice tasks involving delayed monetary rewards, the respondent consistently prefers the larger, later reward over smaller, sooner rewards, even when the delay difference is small. For example, she chooses $6 in 6 weeks over $3 to $7 in 5 weeks, and similarly for other amounts and delays. This pattern indicates high patience and a strong preference for delayed rewards over immediate smaller rewards.

## Social preferences  
In the dictator game, the respondent proposes to keep $2 and give $3 to the other person. In the ultimatum game, she accepts all offers, including the least favorable. In the trust game, she sends the full $5 to the other person and, as receiver, keeps $5 and returns $10 out of $15. These choices indicate a prosocial orientation with generosity and fairness. The respondent’s written statements reinforce this, expressing a strong desire to help others without expecting rewards and prioritizing others’ needs over her own.
