## Intertemporal patience

Across multiple choice matrices comparing amounts available sooner versus later (e.g., $6 in 6 weeks vs. $3 in 5 weeks), the person consistently chooses the delayed larger amount until the amounts become equal or the sooner amount is clearly better, at which point they switch preference. This pattern holds across different amounts and delays, including choices involving $8, $10, and $6 with varying delays.
