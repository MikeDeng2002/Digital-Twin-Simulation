## Bias susceptibility predictions  
Predictable biases from cognitive signals:  
- Probability matching vs. maximizing: The respondent’s lottery choices show a tendency to accept certain amounts below expected value initially but switch to preferring the lottery at higher amounts, indicating some probability matching tendencies. Their moderate cognitive test performance and moderate NFC suggest a mild tendency toward probability matching rather than strict maximizing.  
- Denominator neglect: The respondent correctly interprets probabilities and percentages in financial literacy and probability questions, indicating low denominator neglect.  
- Belief bias in syllogisms: The respondent shows some belief bias, as seen in incorrect syllogistic reasoning where logically valid conclusions are rejected (e.g., Premise 2: Plants do not love water; Conclusion: Plants are not animals — answered "No" when logically it should be "Yes"). This suggests moderate susceptibility to belief bias.  
- Wason matching bias: The respondent correctly identifies cards to test conditional rules (Wason task), indicating low susceptibility to matching bias.

Weakly predictable or not predictable biases (framing effects, anchoring, base-rate neglect, conjunction fallacy, outcome bias, sunk-cost effect, less-is-more effect, omission bias, Allais/ certainty effects, myside bias) cannot be confidently predicted from the available data, consistent with Stanovich & West (2008) limits.
