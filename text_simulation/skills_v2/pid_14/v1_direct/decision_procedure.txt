## Intertemporal patience

Across multiple matrices comparing fixed delayed amounts (e.g., $6 in 6 weeks) to increasing amounts available sooner (e.g., $3 to $7 in 5 weeks), the person consistently chooses the delayed option until the sooner amount equals or exceeds the delayed amount (e.g., choosing $6 in 6 weeks over $5.75 in 5 weeks, but switching to the sooner amount when it reaches $6 or more). This pattern holds across different amounts and delays, including choices involving immediate rewards versus short delays. Similarly, in lottery exchange tasks involving probabilistic gains and losses, the person shows a willingness to accept certain amounts close to or below expected values, indicating moderate risk tolerance and consistent valuation over time. Overall, the responses reflect a patient intertemporal decision style.
