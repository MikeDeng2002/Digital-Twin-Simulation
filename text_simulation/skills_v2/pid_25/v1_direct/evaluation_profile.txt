## Social preferences  
In dictator game scenarios where the respondent decides how to split $5 with an anonymous other, she chooses to keep the entire $5 for herself and send $0 to the other person. However, when playing as the receiver, she accepts all offers from the sender, including the least generous ($0 for sender, $5 for receiver) and the most generous ($5 for sender, $0 for receiver). In the trust game where money sent to the other person is tripled, she chooses to keep $4 and send $1 to the other person as sender, and as receiver, she tends to keep the vast majority of money sent back, sending only small amounts back to the sender. These choices indicate a self-interested allocation preference with low generosity as proposer but acceptance of all offers as responder. The respondent’s thoughts during these decisions emphasize self-interest and reluctance to share with unknown others.
