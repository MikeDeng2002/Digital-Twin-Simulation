- Denominator neglect: Present, as shown by misestimation of probabilities (e.g., disease risk 20/100 answered as 2% instead of 20%).  
- Belief bias: Present, as seen in acceptance of invalid logical conclusions consistent with prior beliefs (e.g., China not communist).  
- Wason matching bias: Likely present, given selection of cards to test conditional rules (selected only A, not the logically necessary cards).

**Moderate confidence predictions:**

- Risk-benefit nonseparability (affect heuristic): Moderate, as participant shows some emotional engagement but also analytical approach.

**Low confidence predictions (acknowledging limits):**  
- Framing effects, anchoring, base-rate neglect, conjunction fallacy, outcome bias, sunk-cost effect, less-is-more effect, omission bias, Allais/ certainty effects, myside bias: These biases are not reliably predictable from the data and cognitive ability alone.
