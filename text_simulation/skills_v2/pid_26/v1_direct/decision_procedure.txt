Regarding cognitive test items, the person answered a variety of logic, reasoning, and CRT-style questions. They correctly answered syllogistic reasoning items such as "If a glock is a XAR..." Overall, the person shows a high motivation to engage in thinking and problem-solving but demonstrates some errors in logical and spatial reasoning tasks, including some intuitive-trap errors in syllogistic reasoning.

## Intertemporal patience
The intertemporal choice tasks show a strong preference for larger, later rewards over smaller, sooner ones. Across multiple choice matrices involving money delayed by weeks versus smaller amounts sooner, the person consistently chooses the delayed option (e.g., preferring $6 in 6 weeks over $3 in 5 weeks, $8 in 7 weeks over $4 in 6 weeks, $10 in 7 weeks over $5 in 5 weeks, etc.). Only when the amounts are equal or the sooner amount is substantially larger do they switch preference to the sooner option.

This pattern indicates a relatively patient intertemporal choice style, favoring delayed gratification and valuing future rewards over immediate smaller gains.

No direct scale evidence for other biases such as anchoring, overconfidence, or framing effects is present in the transcript.

## Summary

Their Need for Closure profile indicates moderate tolerance for uncertainty with a preference for order and structure. They show low to moderate maximization tendencies and a very stable, clear self-concept. Intertemporal choice responses reveal a patient style, favoring delayed rewards. Bias susceptibility evidence is limited but suggests moderate social desirability and good self-monitoring, with some resistance to confirmation bias.
