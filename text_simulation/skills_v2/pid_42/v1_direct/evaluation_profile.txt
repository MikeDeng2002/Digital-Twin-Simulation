## Risk preferences  
The survey includes multiple lottery and risk preference tasks. The respondent consistently prefers the lottery over smaller certain amounts until the certain amount approaches the expected value, indicating moderate risk aversion. For example, she prefers the 50% chance of $6 over certain amounts up to about $2.50, switching preference to the certain amount at $2.50 and above. Similar patterns hold for other lotteries with varying payoffs.  

In loss lotteries, she prefers the lottery over smaller certain losses, indicating risk aversion in losses as well. She rejects offers that involve losing more than about $3 to $4 for certain, preferring the lottery instead.  

In ultimatum and dictator games, she proposes to keep $3 and send $2 out of $5, and as a receiver, she accepts all offers except the one where the sender keeps all $5. In the trust game with tripled amounts, she keeps $2 and sends $3, and as receiver, she tends to keep a bit more than half of the tripled amount but returns a fair share when the sender is generous.  

Overall, these responses indicate moderate risk aversion, some fairness and reciprocity concerns in social economic games, and a willingness to share but also to keep a reasonable share.
