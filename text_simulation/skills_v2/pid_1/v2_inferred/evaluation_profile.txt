## Budget constraint  
The respondent is a female aged 30-49, living in a three-person household in the Midwest, with some college education but no degree, and currently employed part-time. Her total family income is between $50,000 and $75,000. She reports feeling overwhelmed at times and sometimes puts off household tasks due to time constraints (free response). This suggests a moderate budget constraint with some financial and time pressure, likely limiting discretionary spending. The part-time employment and household size imply a moderate spending ceiling for everyday purchases, probably cautious but not severely restricted.

## Values hierarchy  
The values ratings show a strong acquiescence pattern, with most values rated between 7 and 9, indicating compression. Therefore, only relative distinctions are meaningful. The highest rated value is TRUST (9), followed closely by WEALTH, PLEASURE, FORGIVENESS, INFLUENCE, COMPETENCE, AMBITION, LOYALTY, POLITENESS, POWER, HONESTY, COMPASSION, STATUS, and CIVILITY (all 8). Lower but still relatively high values include HUMILITY, ACHIEVEMENT, ALTRUISM, EXCITEMENT, AUTONOMY, EQUALITY, RECOGNITION, TRADITION, and SUPERIORITY (7). This pattern suggests a strong emphasis on social trust, competence, ambition, loyalty, and power, combined with compassion and honesty. The high conscientiousness in Big Five (e.g., "Does a thorough job" 5, "Is a reliable worker" 5, "Perseveres until the task is finished" 5) converges with high COMPETENCE and ACHIEVEMENT values, indicating consistency. The respondent values both personal success and social harmony, with a notable emphasis on loyalty and family duty (see social preferences).

## Minimalism and consumer orientation  
The respondent strongly endorses minimalism (items 1-3 rated 5, and most others 4 or 5), indicating a strong preference for owning fewer, carefully selected possessions and visually sparse environments. Consumerism and uniqueness items show low agreement with seeking uniqueness or breaking social norms in consumption (mostly 2 or 3), with some mild agreement on disliking common brands (items 11 and 12 rated 4). She identifies as a spendthrift (self-rated 11 on spending habits scale) but also reports sometimes having trouble limiting spending and sometimes trouble spending money, indicating ambivalence. She aligns more with cautious spending behavior (prefers Mr. B in shopping scenario). Overall, the strong minimalism orientation will heavily influence her purchasing behavior, favoring simplicity and necessity over indulgence or brand prestige, despite some tendency toward spending difficulty.

## Intertemporal patience  
The respondent consistently prefers delayed larger rewards over smaller sooner ones across multiple intertemporal choice tasks, choosing to wait for higher amounts even when the delay is short (e.g., $6 in 6 weeks over $5.75 in 5 weeks, $8 in 7 weeks over $7.75 in 6 weeks, $10 in 7 weeks over $9.75 in 5 weeks). This indicates relatively high patience. This is consistent with her high conscientiousness traits (e.g., "Does a thorough job," "Is a reliable worker," "Perseveres until the task is finished") and her agreement with statements about liking order and routine. She also agrees strongly that she tries to make work accurate and error-free. Thus, she likely plans ahead and values future benefits over immediate gratification.

## Risk preferences  
In lottery tasks, the respondent shows moderate risk aversion for gains, often preferring certain amounts slightly below expected value rather than lotteries (e.g., prefers $3.25 over 50% chance of $6, but sometimes switches to lottery at higher amounts). For losses, she consistently prefers sure smaller losses over risky larger losses, indicating risk aversion in losses as well. This is typical for moderate-income individuals. Her financial literacy is moderate, with some errors in synonym/antonym tasks and cognitive tests, but she understands basic financial concepts (e.g., mortgage payments, inflation). Overall, she is moderately risk averse, especially for losses, consistent with her moderate income and conscientiousness.

## Social preferences  
The respondent shows a strong family-oriented social preference, rating family loyalty and duty items very highly (items 13-16 on family duty and group respect all rated 5 - agree strongly). She states in free response that she would do anything for her family but admits to an initial selfish impulse in thought. In economic games, she proposes to keep all money for herself when acting as proposer in a dictator game (keeping $5, sending $0), indicating selfish stranger-oriented behavior. However, as a receiver, she accepts all offers, even the least generous, showing pragmatic acceptance. In the trust game, she sends a moderate amount ($2 of $5) to the other person, and when receiving money, she keeps a majority but returns some, indicating moderate reciprocity. This pattern shows a clear stranger-family distinction: selfish and self-protective with strangers, but strong loyalty and self-sacrifice for family.

## Product-category predictions for the pricing study  
Given the strong minimalism orientation, moderate income, and moderate risk aversion, the respondent is likely to be cautious in purchasing, especially at elevated prices. For grocery staples (eggs, milk, bread, basic snacks), she is moderately likely to purchase but may avoid premium or bulk formats due to minimalism and budget constraints. Premium branded items (e.g., Häagen-Dazs) are less likely to be purchased at elevated prices, as she dislikes common brands but also avoids indulgence. Bulk/party-size items are unlikely to be favored due to preference for simplicity and smaller quantities. Indulgence/novelty items (candy, beer, ice cream) are less likely to be purchased at higher prices, consistent with minimalism and moderate income. Household necessities (detergent, batteries) are more likely to be purchased even at elevated prices, as they are essential and align with her conscientiousness and practical orientation.

## Summary  
This respondent is a moderately patient, conscientious female in her 30s-40s with some college education and moderate income, living in a small household. She strongly values trust, competence, loyalty, and family duty, but exhibits a clear selfish stance toward strangers in economic games. She is a strong minimalist, preferring simplicity and careful ownership, which heavily constrains her purchasing behavior despite some tendencies toward spending difficulty. Her risk preferences are moderately risk averse, especially for losses, and she plans ahead with a preference for delayed rewards. Overall, she is likely to avoid premium, indulgence, and bulk purchases at elevated prices, focusing spending on essential household necessities and basic groceries, reflecting a budget-conscious, minimalist, and family-oriented consumer profile.
