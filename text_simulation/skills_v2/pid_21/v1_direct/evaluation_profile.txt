## Budget constraint

These responses provide a direct indication of a moderate to high budget envelope without any inference about spending style.

## Values hierarchy  
The 24-item values scale shows evidence of rating compression, with many values rated highly (6 to 9). The respondent rated WEALTH (item 1), TRUST (5), LOYALTY (11), HARMONY (14), HONESTY (16), and EQUALITY (21) as the highest values, all at 9 ("Highly Important to me"). The lowest-rated values include RECOGNITION (22) at 1 ("Not Important to me"), and low ratings (2 or 3) for INFLUENCE (4), STATUS (18), TRADITION (23), and SUPERIORITY (24). This suggests a relative values structure emphasizing financial success, trustworthiness, loyalty, social harmony, honesty, and equality, while placing little importance on fame, influence, status, tradition, or superiority.

## Minimalism and consumer orientation  
On the minimalism scale (12 items), the respondent generally agrees with minimalist statements, with strong agreement on preferring simplicity in design (item 6) and leaving spaces visually empty (item 8), both rated 5 ("Agree strongly"). Other items mostly received 4 ("Agree a little"), indicating a mild to moderate minimalist orientation. Regarding consumerism and uniqueness-seeking, the respondent mostly disagrees or disagrees a little with statements about seeking uniqueness or breaking social norms in consumption (items 1-8 on consumerism scale), indicating low consumerism and uniqueness-seeking tendencies. She slightly agrees (4) that she uses products less when they become popular (item 9), but overall shows little desire to stand out through consumption.

## Risk preferences  
The survey includes multiple lottery and risk preference tasks. The respondent consistently prefers the lottery option over smaller certain amounts when the certain amount is substantially lower than the expected lottery value, but switches to the certain amount when it approaches or exceeds the expected lottery value. For example, she prefers the lottery over amounts up to about $2.75 when the lottery expected value is $3, but accepts certain amounts at or above $3.00. She is risk averse in gains, as she prefers certain amounts close to or above expected values rather than lotteries. For losses, she consistently prefers the lottery (risk) over certain losses, indicating risk-seeking behavior in losses. In a mixed lottery with a 50% chance of losing $8 and 50% chance of winning various amounts, she prefers the certain $0 option when the winning amount is $7 or less, but prefers the lottery when the winning amount is $8 or more. This pattern is consistent with loss aversion and risk-seeking in losses. Overall, the respondent exhibits moderate risk aversion in gains and risk seeking in losses.

## Intertemporal patience  
The respondent consistently prefers smaller, sooner rewards over larger, later rewards across all intertemporal choice tasks presented. For example, she chooses $3 in 5 weeks over $6 in 6 weeks, and $4.00 now over $6.00 in 1 week, indicating impatience or a high discount rate for delayed rewards. Only when the delayed reward equals or exceeds the immediate reward by a substantial margin does she switch preference to the delayed option (e.g., $6 in 6 weeks vs. $6 now). This pattern suggests relatively low intertemporal patience.

## Social preferences  
In dictator and ultimatum game scenarios, the respondent proposes to keep $2 and send $3 to the other person when splitting $5, indicating a moderately generous offer. When acting as the receiver, she accepts all offers from $0 to $5 sent to her, including the offer where the sender keeps all $5 and sends $0, indicating a high acceptance threshold and possibly a preference for any positive amount. In the trust game where sent amounts are tripled, she chooses to keep $2 and send $3, and when receiving amounts multiplied by three, she tends to keep about two-thirds and return one-third to the sender (e.g., keeps $10 and sends $5 out of $15). Her thoughts during these decisions emphasize kindness, compassion, fairness, and caring. This indicates a social preference profile characterized by moderate generosity, fairness, and reciprocity.

## Summary  
The respondent has a moderate to high budget envelope with full-time employment and a household of two. Her values emphasize wealth, trust, loyalty, harmony, honesty, and equality, while placing little importance on recognition, status, or tradition. She exhibits a mild to moderate minimalist orientation and low consumerism or uniqueness-seeking tendencies. Her risk preferences show moderate risk aversion in gains and risk seeking in losses, consistent with common behavioral patterns. She is relatively impatient in intertemporal choices, favoring immediate rewards. Socially, she demonstrates moderate generosity and fairness in economic games, with a strong acceptance of offers and a reciprocal approach in trust games.
