## Intertemporal patience  
The individual consistently prefers larger, later rewards over smaller, sooner ones, even when the delay difference is small, indicating high patience and low impulsivity in intertemporal choice. For example, they repeatedly choose $6 in 6 weeks over $5.75 in 5 weeks, and $10 in 7 weeks over $9.75 in 5 weeks, only switching preference when the immediate reward equals or exceeds the delayed reward. This pattern shows a stable, patient discounting function.

High conscientiousness and strong planning traits support this patience. Free responses about being "determined" and "organized" align with a future-oriented mindset. There is no evidence of procrastination or impulsive decision-making. Overall, the person exhibits strong intertemporal patience and consistent preference for delayed gratification.

Empathy scores are mixed: they disagree strongly with statements about being easily caught up in others’ feelings but agree a little that they can understand friends’ emotions, suggesting moderate cognitive empathy but low affective empathy. Their moderate social desirability and some admitted social ambivalence may increase cautious projection but not strongly. Overall, moderate egocentric projection tendency.

## Bias susceptibility predictions  

**HIGH CONFIDENCE**  
- Probability matching vs. maximizing: Likely to maximize rather than probability match, given good cognitive test performance and consistent lottery valuation choices favoring maximizing expected value.  
- Denominator neglect: Low likelihood, as the person correctly interprets probabilities and percentages in multiple tasks (e.g., disease risk, lottery odds).  
- Belief bias: Moderate likelihood, given some logical errors in syllogisms (e.g., poodle question), indicating occasional reliance on belief rather than logic.  
- Wason matching bias: Low to moderate likelihood; the person correctly identifies the card to turn over in the Wason task, indicating some resistance to this bias.

**MODERATE CONFIDENCE**  
- False consensus effect: Moderate, due to moderate self-concept clarity and empathy, and independent political identity.  
- Risk-benefit nonseparability (affect heuristic): Moderate, as the person values excitement and pleasure highly but also shows pragmatic risk assessment in lotteries.

**LOW CONFIDENCE**  
- Framing effects, anchoring, base-rate neglect, conjunction fallacy, outcome bias, sunk-cost effect, less-is-more effect, omission bias, Allais/ certainty effects, myside bias: These biases are not reliably predictable from the data and cognitive ability alone, per Stanovich & West (2008). The person’s moderate cognitive ability and pragmatic style suggest some susceptibility but no strong prediction.
