The respondent shows a mild to moderate minimalist orientation. They agree somewhat or strongly with statements about avoiding accumulating excess possessions (items 1, 3, 4), preferring simplicity and sparsity in their environment (items 5-8), and being mindful and thoughtful about what they own (items 9-12). ## Risk preferences  
The survey includes multiple lottery and risk preference tasks. The respondent generally prefers the lottery over smaller certain amounts when the certain amount is substantially less than the expected value, indicating some risk tolerance. For example, they prefer the lottery over certain amounts up to about $1.75 for a 50% chance at $6, switching preference to the certain amount at $2.00 and above. Similar patterns hold for other lotteries with varying payoffs. For lotteries involving losses, the respondent prefers the sure loss over risky losses when the sure loss is less severe, indicating risk aversion in losses. Overall, the respondent exhibits risk aversion for losses and moderate risk tolerance for gains.  

## Intertemporal patience  
The respondent consistently prefers the larger, later reward ($6 in 6 weeks, $8 in 7 weeks, $10 in 7 weeks) over smaller, sooner rewards until the sooner reward equals or exceeds the delayed reward. This pattern holds both for delays of several weeks and for immediate versus short delay choices. This indicates a relatively high degree of patience and willingness to wait for larger rewards rather than preferring immediate smaller rewards.

## Social preferences  
In the dictator/ultimatum game, the respondent proposes to keep $3 and send $2 to the other person, indicating a moderately selfish but not extreme allocation. As a receiver, they accept all offers except the one where the sender keeps all $5, rejecting the $0 offer for themselves. In the trust game where sent amounts are tripled, the respondent keeps $3 and sends $2 as sender, and as receiver, they keep a majority share of the tripled amount (e.g., keep $8 of $15 when sent $5). These choices indicate moderate fairness concerns but a preference for keeping a larger share for themselves. Their stated thoughts during these decisions emphasize fairness and honesty.
