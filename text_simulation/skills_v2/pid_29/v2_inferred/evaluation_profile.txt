## Risk preferences  
The respondent shows moderate risk aversion in gains, preferring certain smaller amounts over lotteries with equal or higher expected value until the certain amount approaches the expected value of the lottery (Lottery tasks: prefers certain amounts below expected value, switches to lottery only when certain amount is low). For losses, the respondent is risk seeking, preferring lotteries with a chance of no loss over certain smaller losses (loss lotteries: prefers lotteries over certain losses). This gain/loss asymmetry is typical. Given low income, moderate risk aversion in gains is normative. The respondent’s financial literacy and cognitive skills support informed risk assessment.
