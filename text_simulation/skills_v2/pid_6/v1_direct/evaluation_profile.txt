## Intertemporal patience  
The respondent consistently prefers the smaller, later reward ($6 in 6 weeks) over larger, sooner rewards up to $5.75 in 5 weeks, switching preference only when the sooner reward equals or exceeds the delayed reward. This pattern holds across multiple delayed reward comparisons, indicating a relatively high degree of patience and preference for delayed rewards unless the sooner reward is substantially better. This suggests moderate to high intertemporal patience.

## Social preferences

In the trust game, the respondent keeps $4 and sends $1 to the other person initially, but when in the receiver role, they keep a majority of the tripled amount but return a meaningful share, indicating moderate trust and reciprocity. These choices reflect a social preference for fairness, reciprocity, and moderate generosity.
