## Intertemporal patience
The intertemporal choice tasks show a preference for immediate rewards over delayed ones, indicating relatively low patience. For example, the person consistently chooses the smaller, sooner reward over the larger, later reward in early comparisons (e.g., preferring $3 in 5 weeks over $6 in 6 weeks, $4 in 6 weeks over $8 in 7 weeks). Only when the delayed reward becomes substantially larger do they switch preference (e.g., choosing $5.50 in 5 weeks over $6 in 6 weeks). This pattern is consistent across multiple monetary amounts and delays, showing a discounting of future rewards and moderate impatience.

No direct evidence was provided for social desirability bias or other specific biases.
