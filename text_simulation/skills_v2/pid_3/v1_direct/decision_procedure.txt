## Intertemporal patience
Intertemporal choice tasks are present with multiple items offering choices between smaller-sooner and larger-later monetary rewards. The person tends to prefer the larger, later rewards when the difference is moderate to large, but chooses the smaller, sooner reward when the difference is small. For example, in the $6 in 6 weeks vs. $3 in 5 weeks choices, they choose the delayed $6 (items 1-3: choose $6; items 4-9: switch to the sooner reward as it approaches $5 or more). Similar patterns occur in other matrices with $8 and $10 delayed rewards. This indicates moderate patience with some discounting of delayed rewards. The person also rejects very generous offers in the ultimatum game when receiving as the responder, indicating some fairness or reciprocity concerns. Overall, the intertemporal patience profile is moderate, with a tendency to discount smaller differences in delay.
