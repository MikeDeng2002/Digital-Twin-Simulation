## Intertemporal patience  
The respondent shows moderate intertemporal patience. In delayed reward tasks, they prefer immediate smaller rewards when the difference is small but switch to delayed larger rewards as the delayed amount increases, indicating sensitivity to reward magnitude and delay. For example, they choose $3 now over $6 in 1 week but switch to $6 in 6 weeks over smaller immediate amounts when the difference is larger.  

Conscientiousness items (e.g., "Makes plans and follows through" 4 - Agree a little; "Does a thorough job" 5 - Agree strongly) support the ability to plan and delay gratification. However, the respondent’s choices suggest some impatience or discounting of delayed rewards, consistent with a moderate level of patience rather than strong long-term orientation.
