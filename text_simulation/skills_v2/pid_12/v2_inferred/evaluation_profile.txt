## Intertemporal patience  
The respondent consistently chooses delayed larger rewards over smaller sooner ones until the delayed reward equals or exceeds the immediate reward (Intertemporal choice items). This indicates moderate to high patience and willingness to wait for better outcomes. This aligns with high Conscientiousness (thorough, reliable, persevering) and preference for order and planning (agreeing strongly with rules, obligations, and structured life). The respondent is likely to plan and delay gratification in financial and consumption decisions.

## Social preferences  
The respondent’s values and behavior indicate strong family-oriented social preferences: high ratings on Loyalty, Altruism, Compassion, and statements endorsing family duty and sacrifice (items 14, 15 rated 5). The respondent accepts all offers except the one where the other keeps all the money, showing moderate fairness toward strangers but prioritizing self-interest.
