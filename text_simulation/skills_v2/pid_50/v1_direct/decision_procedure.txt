The Need for Closure (NFCC) scale responses indicate a relatively high need for closure and low tolerance for uncertainty. The participant strongly agrees with items such as "I don’t like situations that are uncertain" (Item 1, Agree strongly), "I find that a well ordered life with regular hours suits my temperament" (Item 3, Agree strongly), "I don’t like to go into a situation without knowing what I can expect from it" (Item 6, Agree strongly), and "When I have made a decision, I feel relieved" (Item 7, Agree strongly). They also agree strongly that they dislike unpredictable situations and enjoy having a clear and structured mode of life (Items 12 and 13, Agree strongly). 

There is some mild disagreement with items indicating intolerance of differing opinions or ambiguity, such as "I feel irritated when one person disagrees with what everyone else in a group believes" (Item 5, Disagree a little), suggesting that while the participant prefers closure, they may not be rigidly intolerant of dissent.

The Maximizer scale responses show a strong maximizing tendency. The participant strongly agrees with "I often find it difficult to shop for a gift for a friend" (Item 3, Agree strongly), "No matter what I do, I have the highest standards for myself" (Item 5, Agree strongly), and "I never settle for second best" (Item 6, Agree strongly). The participant strongly disagrees with items suggesting satisficing or contentment with average options (Items 1 and 2, Disagree strongly). ## Intertemporal patience
The participant’s choices in intertemporal choice tasks consistently favor the smaller, sooner rewards over larger, later rewards until the delayed reward becomes equal or better. For example, they prefer $6 in 6 weeks over $3 in 5 weeks (Item 1), $8 in 7 weeks over $4 in 6 weeks (Item 1), and $10 in 7 weeks over $5 in 5 weeks (Item 1), but switch preference when the delayed reward equals or exceeds the immediate reward (e.g., $6 in 6 weeks vs. $6 in 5 weeks, choosing the sooner). This pattern is consistent across multiple amounts and delays, indicating moderate to low patience for delayed rewards and a preference for immediacy unless the delayed reward is substantially better.
