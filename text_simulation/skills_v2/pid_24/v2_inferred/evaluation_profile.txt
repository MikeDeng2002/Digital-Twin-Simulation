## Intertemporal patience  
The respondent consistently prefers smaller, sooner rewards over larger, later rewards in delayed-reward tasks, switching to the delayed option only when the difference is substantial (e.g., choosing $6 in 6 weeks over $5 in 5 weeks, but switching when the immediate reward approaches the delayed reward). This indicates relatively low intertemporal patience or high present bias. This is consistent with low Conscientiousness (disorganized, low perseverance) and self-reports of acting on impulse. The respondent’s free responses about needing to be more frugal and determined suggest awareness of this limitation but difficulty overcoming it.

The respondent’s choices indicate moderate risk aversion overall, consistent with low income and financial pressure. The respondent’s choices in economic games (keeping all money for self, rejecting unfair offers) also reflect self-protective, risk-averse behavior.

Overall, the respondent will favor mainstream, affordable, and necessary products, avoiding discretionary or luxury purchases.
