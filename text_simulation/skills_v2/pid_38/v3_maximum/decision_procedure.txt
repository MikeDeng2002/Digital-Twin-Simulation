They also disagree with preferring complex problems or intellectual challenges. This aligns with their Big Five responses showing moderate Conscientiousness (agreeing "Does a thorough job," "Is a reliable worker," "Perseveres until the task is finished," and "Makes plans and follows through with them" at "Agree a little" rather than strongly). The cognitive test performance reveals a pattern of specific logical reasoning errors: notably, the participant incorrectly rejects logically valid conclusions in syllogistic reasoning tasks (e.g., denying that "All animals love water; plants do not; therefore plants are not animals" follows logically), indicating a belief bias or difficulty with abstract logical inference. They also failed to identify the correct cube rotation and opted for "I do not know" repeatedly, suggesting either uncertainty or avoidance in spatial reasoning tasks. Their vocabulary synonym and antonym choices are mixed, with some errors (e.g., choosing "harass" as synonym for "confiscate," "entrance" for "solicit," and "facile" for "furtive") indicating some semantic confusion or guessing. Overall, the cognitive style is characterized by moderate conscientiousness and effort regulation but a tendency to avoid deep or abstract thinking, with specific logical reasoning errors and some uncertainty in spatial tasks. Free responses emphasize a practical, self-focused thinking style ("What is the most I can get," "Don't think about the other person"), reinforcing a pragmatic rather than analytical approach.

## Closure and uncertainty tolerance

The participant shows moderate Need for Closure (NFCC), agreeing somewhat with statements like "I don’t like situations that are uncertain," "I dislike questions which could be answered in many different ways," and "I don’t like to go into a situation without knowing what I can expect from it." They also agree that "establishing a consistent routine enables me to enjoy life more" and "I enjoy having a clear and structured mode of life," indicating a preference for order and predictability. However, they neither strongly agree nor disagree with impatience or urgency to resolve problems immediately, suggesting some tolerance for ambiguity but a general preference for closure. Their free responses and behavioral choices in economic games (e.g., keeping all money for themselves, rejecting unfair offers) suggest a cautious, self-protective stance rather than openness to risk or uncertainty. The participant’s moderate NFCC combined with a practical, somewhat risk-averse approach indicates they prefer to reduce ambiguity but do not exhibit extreme intolerance of uncertainty.

They also report rarely having trouble limiting spending but always having trouble spending money, consistent with a tightwad profile that prioritizes frugality over indulgence. Overall, the optimization strategy is pragmatic, self-focused, and oriented toward securing maximum personal benefit with minimal cognitive effort or indecision.

## Intertemporal patience

The participant shows low intertemporal patience, consistently preferring smaller, sooner rewards over larger, later ones in delayed reward tasks, even when the delayed reward is substantially better. This is evident in their repeated choice of immediate smaller amounts over delayed larger amounts across multiple time horizons. This impatience aligns with their moderate Conscientiousness (agreeing a little with "Makes plans and follows through," but not strongly) and their free responses emphasizing practical, immediate gain. They also report rarely procrastinating or having difficulty making decisions, indicating some planning ability, but their economic choices suggest a preference for immediate gratification. Overall, intertemporal patience is low to moderate, with a bias toward immediacy and short-term reward.

## Egocentric projection tendency

The participant’s moderate Self-Concept Clarity and mixed Empathy scores (agreeing somewhat with being affected by others’ emotions but also disagreeing with some empathy items) suggest a moderate level of egocentric projection. Their political identity is moderate (Independent, conservative-leaning), which may increase projection on political or policy judgments. The guardedness in self-disclosure and the self-focused economic game behavior further support a moderate false consensus effect, where the participant may overestimate the similarity of others to their own self-interested preferences. Thus, egocentric projection tendency is moderate, with some potential for false consensus bias especially in domains related to competition, fairness, and social cooperation.

- **Probability matching vs. maximizing:** Likely to probability match or show inconsistent maximizing, given their pragmatic but not deeply analytical style and some errors in probabilistic reasoning (e.g., underestimating expected frequencies in die rolls).

- **Dominator (denominator) neglect:** Moderate likelihood, as seen in some misestimation of probabilities and frequencies (e.g., incorrect percentage for 1 in 1,000 as 10%).

- **Belief bias:** High likelihood, given their consistent rejection of logically valid conclusions when they conflict with intuitive beliefs.

- **Wason matching bias:** Likely present, as indicated by their choice of cards to test conditional rules focusing on confirming rather than falsifying evidence.

**MODERATE CONFIDENCE:**

- **False consensus effect:** Moderate, due to moderate SCC, empathy, and political identity strength.

- **Risk-benefit nonseparability (affect heuristic):** Moderate, given their risk-averse economic choices and preference for immediate gains.

**LOW CONFIDENCE (acknowledging predictive limits):**

- Framing effects, anchoring, base-rate neglect, conjunction fallacy, outcome bias, sunk-cost effect, less-is-more effect, omission bias, Allais/ certainty effects, myside bias — these are not reliably predictable from the data.

## Summary

They tend to avoid deep or abstract thinking, as shown by specific logical reasoning errors and uncertainty in spatial tasks. Their preference for closure and order is moderate, favoring predictability but tolerating some ambiguity. A pronounced gap exists between their stated prosocial values and revealed self-interested economic behavior, suggesting impression management or aspirational self-presentation. They display low intertemporal patience, favoring immediate rewards, and moderate egocentric projection, which may bias social judgments. The most predictive feature of their reasoning profile is the clear belief bias in logical reasoning tasks combined with a pragmatic, self-maximizing approach in economic decisions, indicating a reasoning style that prioritizes intuitive, self-serving conclusions over normative logic or cooperative fairness.
