## Intertemporal patience  
The respondent consistently prefers larger delayed rewards over smaller sooner ones until the difference becomes quite large (Intertemporal choice tasks). For example, they choose $6 in 6 weeks over $5.50 in 5 weeks but switch preference when the sooner amount reaches $5.50 or more. This pattern indicates moderate to high patience and willingness to wait for larger rewards. This is consistent with very high Conscientiousness (items 3, 13, 28, 33, 38 rated 5) and high planning capacity (item 38 "Makes plans and follows through" rated 5). The respondent shows a clear preference for delayed gratification and planning.

## Risk preferences  
Lottery tasks show the respondent is risk averse for gains, preferring certain amounts below expected value over lotteries (e.g., preferring $2.50 or less over a 50% chance at $6). For losses, the respondent is also risk averse, preferring smaller certain losses over lotteries with larger potential losses. This is typical for moderate to high income individuals. The respondent’s financial literacy is high, understanding risk and diversification well. There is no strong gain/loss asymmetry beyond typical risk aversion. Overall, the respondent is moderately risk averse.

## Social preferences  
The respondent’s social preferences show a nuanced pattern. Values ratings show high altruism, compassion, loyalty, and politeness (8-9 ratings), indicating strong family- and group-oriented social preferences. Behavioral game responses show moderate selfishness toward strangers: in the dictator game, the respondent proposes to keep $3 and send $2 to the other person, and would accept offers down to $2 for the other person but reject offers where the other keeps $4 or $5. This indicates moderate generosity but a clear self-interest bias toward strangers. The respondent also reports strong family duty and loyalty (items 13-15 in social preferences rated 4-5). Thus, the respondent is generous and loyal within family and close groups but more self-interested with strangers, preserving the stranger-vs-family distinction.

## Product-category predictions for the pricing study  
Given the strong minimalism orientation combined with moderate to high income and moderate risk aversion, the respondent is likely to be selective in purchases, avoiding unnecessary or indulgent spending. Grocery staples and household necessities are likely to be purchased regularly, but the respondent may be sensitive to elevated prices, preferring to buy only what is needed. Premium branded items are less likely to be purchased at elevated prices due to minimalism and low emphasis on status or recognition. Bulk/party-size items may be less favored unless they align with efficiency or value. Indulgence/novelty items (candy, beer, ice cream) are likely to be purchased less frequently and less likely at elevated prices, consistent with minimalism and moderate pleasure value. Overall, the respondent will prioritize functional, necessary purchases, avoid excess, and be price sensitive, especially for non-essential or premium products.
