## Intertemporal patience
Intertemporal choice tasks are present and directly measured. The respondent consistently prefers the larger, later monetary rewards over smaller, sooner ones when the amounts are close (e.g., choosing $6 in 6 weeks over $5.25 in 5 weeks, but switching preference when the sooner amount is $5.25 or higher). Their choices in loss lotteries similarly show risk aversion and preference for smaller certain losses over probabilistic larger losses.
