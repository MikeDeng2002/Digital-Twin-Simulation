## Effort regulation and cognitive style

This individual exhibits a moderately high Need for Cognition (NFC), agreeing strongly with statements such as liking responsibility that requires a lot of thinking and preferring complex problems, but also showing ambivalence or neutrality toward enjoying abstract thinking and intellectual challenge. Cognitive test performance is mixed but generally competent. The person correctly answered classic trick questions (e.g., "Emily’s father has three daughters" answered as "Emily," and "How many cubic feet of dirt in a hole" answered as 27), indicating good attention to linguistic cues and logical reasoning. However, there are notable errors in synonym and antonym tasks, with some incorrect choices revealing occasional semantic confusion or surface-level processing (e.g., choosing "heinous" as synonym for "furtive," "lineal" for "astute," and "arduous" for "indolent"). This pattern suggests occasional lapses in semantic precision or overreliance on superficial word features.

In spatial and pattern reasoning tasks (cube rotations, matrix completions), the participant shows solid but not flawless performance, correctly solving most puzzles but making a few questionable choices (e.g., rejecting valid cube rotations). This indicates a generally analytical style with some difficulty in complex spatial transformations or in integrating multiple visual features simultaneously.

Big Five responses show moderate Conscientiousness: the person agrees "a little" to "does a thorough job," "is a reliable worker," "perseveres until the task is finished," and "does things efficiently," but also admits to being "somewhat careless" and "easily distracted." This suggests a conscientiousness tempered by occasional lapses in focus or care.

Free responses reveal a self-description as an "anxious perfectionist" who "puts off taking care of myself to complete tasks to the best of my ability" and "gets overwhelmed easily," indicating a cognitive style that is effortful but prone to stress and possibly rumination. The person aspires to be more organized and a good leader, reflecting a goal-oriented and self-critical mindset.

Overall, the reasoning style is moderately analytical, with a pragmatic approach to cognitive effort, some semantic imprecision, and a tendency toward perfectionism that may sometimes interfere with smooth cognitive processing.

Free responses reinforce this: the person feels overwhelmed easily and struggles to relax, which may reflect low tolerance for uncertainty or ambiguity in daily life. They also describe feeling pressured to be "perfect" and well-organized, which aligns with a desire for closure and control.

In sum, this person has a moderate intolerance of uncertainty, seeking closure and structure but not to an extreme degree. They likely prefer clear, decisive answers and may become impatient or anxious when faced with ambiguity or open-ended problems.

Overall, the person exhibits a strong maximizing strategy focused on personal benefit and high standards, with a cautious, self-interested approach to social-economic decisions and a preference for thorough evaluation rather than satisficing.
