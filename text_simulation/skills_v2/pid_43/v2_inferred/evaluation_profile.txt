## Intertemporal patience  
The respondent consistently prefers larger delayed rewards over smaller sooner ones until the difference becomes substantial, indicating moderate patience. For example, they prefer $6 in 6 weeks over $5.50 in 5 weeks but switch preference when the sooner amount reaches $5.50 or more. This pattern repeats across different amounts and delays, showing a balanced but not extreme discounting of future rewards. This aligns with moderate Conscientiousness (e.g., "Does a thorough job" and "Is a reliable worker" rated 5, but "Perseveres until the task is finished" rated 3). The respondent aspires to discipline and resilience but admits some struggles with procrastination, consistent with moderate intertemporal patience.

## Risk preferences  
In lottery tasks, the respondent shows moderate risk aversion for gains, preferring certain amounts below expected values until the certain amount approaches or exceeds the expected value. For example, they reject low certain amounts in exchange for a 50% chance at $6 but accept certain amounts closer to or above expected values. For losses, the respondent is strongly loss averse, preferring to avoid lotteries with potential losses even when the alternative loss is larger, indicating a strong preference to avoid losses. This is consistent with moderate household income and student status, where risk aversion is normative. The respondent’s choices in hypothetical scenarios also show sensitivity to losses and gains, with a preference for certainty and aversion to unfair offers.
