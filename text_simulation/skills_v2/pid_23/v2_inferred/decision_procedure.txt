## Optimization strategy  
The Maximizer scale responses indicate a tendency toward maximizing behavior, with strong agreement on "I never settle for second best" (5) and "No matter what I do, I have the highest standards for myself" (4). The respondent also agrees moderately with difficulty in gift shopping and finding clothing they really love (4), suggesting some domain-specific maximizing tendencies in consumer behavior.  

However, the respondent also shows some satisficing or moderate behavior in other domains, such as a moderate score on "No matter how satisfied I am with my job, it’s only right for me to be on the lookout for better opportunities" (4), indicating a balanced approach rather than relentless maximizing.  

In economic games, the respondent proposes to keep $3 and send $2 in the dictator game, which is a moderate offer rather than a fully maximizing or fully generous split. In the trust game, they send the full $5 but expect to keep $7 of the $15 returned, indicating strategic maximizing behavior in reciprocity.  

Thus, the respondent maximizes in personal standards and strategic social exchanges but satisficing or moderate in some consumer and interpersonal domains. This split suggests a pragmatic maximizer profile.
