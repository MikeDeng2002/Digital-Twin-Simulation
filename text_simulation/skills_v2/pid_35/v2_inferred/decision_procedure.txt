This may reduce the reliability of self-report measures alone for predicting behavior under uncertainty, highlighting the importance of behavioral data.  

## Intertemporal patience  
The participant consistently prefers smaller-sooner rewards over larger-later rewards until the delayed reward is equal or clearly better, indicating relatively low patience or a preference for immediacy. This is consistent across multiple intertemporal choice matrices.  

Conscientiousness items (e.g., "Makes plans and follows through with them" 4 - Agree a little; "Organized" 7 on 9-point scale) suggest moderate planning ability, but the intertemporal choices indicate a moderate discounting of future rewards.  

Overall, the participant shows moderate to low intertemporal patience, favoring immediate rewards unless delayed rewards are substantially better.  

## Bias susceptibility predictions  
Predictable from cognitive signals:  

- Probability matching vs. maximizing: Given moderate Need for Cognition and cognitive test performance, the participant is likely to lean toward maximizing rather than probability matching in decision tasks. Their logical reasoning and consistent choices support this.  

- Dominator (denominator) neglect: The participant correctly interprets probabilities and percentages in lottery and risk questions, indicating low susceptibility to denominator neglect.  

- Belief bias in syllogisms: The participant correctly rejects invalid syllogisms and accepts valid ones, showing low belief bias.  

- Wason matching bias: The participant correctly identifies cards to test conditional rules (cards A and 3), indicating low Wason matching bias.  

Weakly predictable or not predictable from cognitive signals:  

- Framing effects, anchoring and adjustment, base-rate neglect, conjunction fallacy, outcome bias, sunk-cost effect, less-is-more effect, omission bias, Allais/certainty effects, and myside bias cannot be confidently predicted from the available data. The participant’s moderate cognitive style and values do not provide strong evidence for or against susceptibility to these biases.  

## Summary

They show a mild preference for closure and order, committing relatively quickly once decisions are made, and a satisficing optimization strategy rather than maximizing. Self-concept is relatively stable, with some acknowledged gaps between aspirational and actual self, especially regarding patience and altruism. Economic game behavior reveals a moderate generosity that does not fully align with highly altruistic stated values, indicating a behavioral vs. stated-preference gap. Intertemporal choices show moderate impatience, favoring immediate rewards unless delayed rewards are substantially better. Cognitive test performance predicts low susceptibility to probability matching, denominator neglect, belief bias, and Wason matching bias, while other biases remain unpredictable from the data.
