Overall, the respondent shows reasonable intertemporal patience and planning capacity, favoring delayed rewards but not excessively so.

## Social preferences  
The respondent’s values and behavioral game responses show a strong family-oriented social preference. Loyalty, tradition, and family duty are rated highly (9), and the respondent strongly agrees that parents and children must stay together and that family members should stick together even at personal sacrifice. In economic games, the respondent proposes to keep a majority share for herself but still offers a substantial amount to strangers ($3 out of $5), and accepts all offers from others, indicating moderate generosity to strangers but with a self-interested tilt. When receiving money multiplied by others, she keeps a fair but not maximal share, showing reciprocity and fairness. This pattern preserves a strong family-collectivism combined with moderate selfishness toward strangers, consistent with the stranger-vs-family distinction.
