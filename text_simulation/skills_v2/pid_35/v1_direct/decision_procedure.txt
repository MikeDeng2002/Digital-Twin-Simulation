## Intertemporal patience
The intertemporal choice tasks show a consistent preference for larger, later rewards over smaller, sooner rewards until the amounts become equal or clearly better. For example, the person consistently chose $6 in 6 weeks over $3 to $5.75 in 5 weeks (items 1-7), switching to the sooner reward only when it matched or exceeded $6 (items 8-9). Similar patterns hold for $8 in 7 weeks and $10 in 7 weeks, with preference for the delayed reward until the immediate reward is equal or better. This pattern is consistent across multiple delays and amounts, indicating moderate patience and willingness to wait for larger rewards. The person also accepted all offers in the ultimatum game scenarios, even when the proposer kept $5 and sent $0, indicating low rejection of unfair offers.
