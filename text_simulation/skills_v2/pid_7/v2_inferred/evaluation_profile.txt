## Intertemporal patience  
The respondent shows moderate to high patience in intertemporal choice tasks, often preferring delayed larger rewards over smaller sooner ones, especially when the difference is substantial. She switches preference to sooner rewards only when the immediate amount approaches the delayed amount. This indicates moderate intertemporal patience. Cross-referenced with her moderately high Conscientiousness (e.g., "Does a thorough job" 5, "Organized" 8, "Perseveres" 4), this suggests reasonable planning capacity and self-control in financial decisions.

## Risk preferences  
Lottery task responses indicate risk aversion for gains, preferring certain smaller amounts over lotteries with equal or higher expected value until the certain amount approaches the lottery expected value. For losses, she consistently prefers to avoid lotteries and accepts smaller certain losses, indicating strong loss aversion. This pattern is typical for moderate-income individuals and aligns with her reported income and unemployment status. No strong gain/loss asymmetry beyond typical loss aversion is evident.

## Social preferences

She rates Compassion (9) and Honesty (9) highly and shows strong empathy in self-reports (e.g., "I often become sad when watching sad things," "I can usually tell when others consider a joke in bad taste"). This indicates a gap between generous family-oriented values and selfish stranger-oriented allocation behavior, consistent with the stranger-vs-family distinction rule.

She is not minimalist but prefers carefully curated, unique products and challenges social norms in consumption. Her spending is cautious but she struggles to limit spending, indicating tension between desire and budget constraints.
