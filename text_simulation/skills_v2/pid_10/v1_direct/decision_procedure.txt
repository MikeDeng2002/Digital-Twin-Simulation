## Intertemporal patience
Intertemporal choice tasks are present with multiple items offering choices between smaller-sooner and larger-later monetary rewards. The person consistently prefers the larger, later rewards when the amounts are close or the delay difference is small, switching to the sooner option only when the sooner reward is substantially smaller (e.g., preferring $6 in 6 weeks over $5.75 in 5 weeks, but switching to $6 in 1 week over $7 now). They also reject offers to keep all money for themselves in ultimatum and trust games, proposing fair splits (e.g., keeping $2 and sending $3), and accept fair and moderately unfair offers as receivers, indicating social preferences consistent with fairness and reciprocity.
