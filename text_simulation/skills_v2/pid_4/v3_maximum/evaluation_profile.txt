## Intertemporal patience  
The person consistently prefers smaller-sooner rewards over larger-later ones when delays are short (weeks), indicating present bias and hyperbolic discounting tendencies. However, when delays are longer or amounts larger, the person switches to preferring larger-later rewards, showing some patience and planning ability. This pattern aligns with moderate intertemporal patience but with a bias toward immediacy in near-term decisions. High conscientiousness and planning orientation suggest the person can override impulsivity when motivated. Free responses and personality data show strong planning and follow-through, indicating behavioral patience may exceed stated impatience in some contexts. Overall, the person is moderately patient with some present bias. Confidence is moderate to high.
