For losses, the respondent consistently prefers to avoid lotteries with losses, choosing safer options, indicating loss aversion. The respondent’s financial literacy is high (correct answers on financial questions, e.g., mortgage, inflation, diversification), supporting informed risk-taking. ## Product-category predictions for the pricing study  
Given the strong minimalism, moderate to high income, and moderate risk aversion, the respondent is likely to be selective in purchases, favoring necessity and quality over indulgence or bulk buying. Grocery staples and household necessities are likely to be purchased but with sensitivity to price increases due to minimalism. Bulk/party-size items are less likely to be favored due to preference for minimal possessions and mindful consumption. Overall, the respondent will likely pay for quality and necessity but avoid excess or premium indulgences unless justified by value or utility.
