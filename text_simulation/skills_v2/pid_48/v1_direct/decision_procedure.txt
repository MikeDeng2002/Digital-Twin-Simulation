## Intertemporal patience
The intertemporal choice tasks show a preference for larger, later rewards over smaller, sooner ones, indicating patience. For example, the person consistently chooses to wait for $6 in 6 weeks rather than $3 to $5.75 in 5 weeks (answers 1 for items 1-7, then 2 for 8-9), and similarly prefers $8 in 7 weeks over smaller amounts in 6 weeks until the amounts equal or exceed $8 (answers 1 for items 1-8, then 2 for 9-10). For $10 in 7 weeks, they prefer the delayed reward until the immediate amount reaches $8 (answers 1 for items 1-3, then 2 for 4-11). For immediate vs. now choices, they prefer waiting for the larger amount until the immediate amount reaches about $5 (answers 1 for items 1-3, then 2 for 4-9). For losses, they consistently prefer the sure smaller loss over lotteries with larger potential losses (answers 2 throughout). For mixed lotteries with losses and gains, they prefer the lottery over the sure $0 option (answers 1 throughout). This pattern indicates moderate to high intertemporal patience and risk aversion for losses.
