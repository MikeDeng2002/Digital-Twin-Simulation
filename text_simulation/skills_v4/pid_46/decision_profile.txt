## Summary  
This participant exhibits a decision-making profile characterized by moderate tightwad spending tendencies and a satisficing search style, avoiding exhaustive option search. Their very high need for cognitive closure drives them to prefer quick, definite decisions and to avoid ambiguity. Regulatory focus leans toward prevention, making them more sensitive to loss and safety framing than to gains or opportunities. They are moderately conscientious and underconfident about their cognitive abilities, believing they perform worse than peers. Time preferences reveal impatience and moderate present bias, favoring immediate rewards. The most predictive feature for simulating this person’s choices is their high need for cognitive closure combined with a prevention-focused regulatory orientation, which together shape their cautious, decisive, and loss-averse decision style.
