## Summary  
This participant exhibits a moderately tightwad spending style, showing some difficulty spending but generally able to limit expenditures. They tend to search for good options but do not maximize exhaustively, balancing effort and sufficiency. Their high need for cognitive closure suggests they prefer quick, decisive choices and dislike ambiguity. A promotion-focused regulatory style dominates, making them more responsive to gain-framed opportunities and achievement-oriented goals. Their conscientiousness is moderate, supporting systematic but not rigid decision execution. Metacognitively, they are moderately overconfident and believe they outperform peers, though their cognitive ability is above average, mitigating concerns about miscalibration. Time preferences reveal impatience and present bias, favoring immediate rewards but willing to wait for sufficiently larger gains. The most predictive feature for simulating this person’s choices is their promotion-focused regulatory orientation, which will strongly influence their responsiveness to framing and opportunity cues.
