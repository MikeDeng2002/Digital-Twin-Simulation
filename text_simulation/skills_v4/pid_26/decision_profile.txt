## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 400% (derived from consistent preference for sooner-smaller rewards over later-larger rewards across all three price lists, with switching only at the highest delayed amounts). The participant always preferred the sooner-smaller reward until the delayed reward was substantially larger, indicating a highly impatient discount rate. This rate is well above typical US adult rates (20%-200%), situating this participant as very impatient in intertemporal trade-offs.

The participant consistently chose the sooner option in all three price lists until the delayed reward was significantly better, showing a strong preference for immediate gratification and low patience for waiting.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: Approximately +0.5 (positive, indicating present bias). The participant shows a moderate present bias, preferring immediate rewards more than the base discount rate predicts. This is directionally consistent with the tightwad spending mode, as both reflect cautious, immediate-focused decision tendencies.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a tightly controlled spending mode, tending toward cautiousness and reluctance to spend freely. Their decision style is satisficing rather than maximizing, with a moderate need for cognitive closure that balances decisiveness and tolerance for ambiguity. A strong promotion focus drives their orientation toward gains and opportunities, making them more responsive to positive framing in choices. They are highly conscientious, extremely organized and efficient in decision execution. Metacognitively, they are underconfident and underestimate their cognitive abilities, which aligns with their below-average cognitive test performance. Their time preferences reveal a very high discount rate and moderate present bias, indicating impatience and a preference for immediate rewards. The most predictive single feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their very high discount rate, which strongly shapes their impatience and time trade-off behavior.
