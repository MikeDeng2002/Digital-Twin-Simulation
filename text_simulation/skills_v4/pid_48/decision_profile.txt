## Summary  
This participant exhibits a moderately tight spending style, cautious about spending but not extremely so. They are a strong maximizer, searching exhaustively for the best options, and have a moderate need for cognitive closure, balancing decisiveness with some tolerance for ambiguity. Their regulatory focus is strongly promotion-oriented, making them more responsive to gain-framed opportunities and ideal-driven goals. They are highly conscientious and organized in decision execution. Metacognitively, they are highly overconfident and overplace themselves relative to peers despite high cognitive ability, indicating a notable miscalibration. Their time preferences reveal moderate impatience and present bias, consistent with their spending caution. The most predictive single feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their strong promotion focus, which will shape their responsiveness to gain versus loss framing and opportunity-seeking behavior.
