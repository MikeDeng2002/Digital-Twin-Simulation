## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 150% (estimated from switching points). The participant switches from sooner-smaller to later-larger rewards at relatively high thresholds, indicating impatience but not extreme. They prefer sooner rewards when the difference is small but switch to later rewards as the delayed amount increases. This rate is on the higher side compared to typical US adults (20%-200%), suggesting moderately high impatience in intertemporal trade-offs.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: Approximately +0.3 (positive). The participant shows a moderate present bias, preferring immediate rewards more than predicted by their base discount rate. This is consistent with their tightwad spending mode, as present bias often correlates with cautious or restrained spending and difficulty delaying gratification.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a cautious and restrained spending style, tending toward tightwad behavior with difficulty spending money comfortably. Their decision style is satisficing rather than maximizing, with a moderately high need for cognitive closure that drives quick decisions and a preference for certainty. They are moderately promotion-focused, motivated by ambition and achievement, and respond more to gain-framed opportunities. Despite low self-rated conscientiousness and self-reported disorganization, their actual cognitive ability is high, though they underestimate their performance and believe they are below average, indicating a significant underconfidence and social comparison bias. Their time preferences reveal moderate impatience and present bias, consistent with their spending caution. The most predictive feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their regulatory focus toward promotion, which shapes their responsiveness to gains and opportunities despite their low confidence and impatience.
