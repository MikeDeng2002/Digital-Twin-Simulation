### Ultimatum Game  
As sender, the participant offers the entire $5 to the other person (0% kept, 100% sent), the most generous possible offer. As receiver, they reject offers of $5, $4, and $3 kept by the sender (i.e., offers of $0, $1, and $2 to them), but accept offers where the sender keeps $3, $4, or $5 (i.e., offers of $2, $1, and $0 to them). The minimum acceptable offer is thus $2 to self (sender keeping $3). This reveals a fairness norm that is extremely generous in giving but relatively intolerant in receiving low offers, rejecting even the most generous offers (0% and 20% to self). There is a strong asymmetry: the participant offers maximal fairness but demands a meaningful fairness floor as receiver.

### Trust Game  
As sender, the participant sends $3 of $5 (60%) to the other person, which will be tripled to $9. As receiver, the participant returns the following amounts for each received amount:  
- Received $15 (sent $5 x 3): returns $5 (33%)  
- Received $12 (sent $4 x 3): returns $7 (58%)  
- Received $9  (sent $3 x 3): returns $5 (56%)  
- Received $6  (sent $2 x 3): returns $3 (50%)  
- Received $3  (sent $1 x 3): returns $2 (67%)  

The participant does not apply a perfectly consistent proportional rule but tends to return roughly between one-third and two-thirds of the amount received, with a tendency to return about half or more for mid-range amounts.  

Participant’s thoughts (sender): "fairness," "generosity," "kindness," "helping," "karma," "caring."  
Participant’s thoughts (receiver): "generousity," "caring," "honesty," "kindness," "hope," "good will."  

This indicates moderate trust in anonymous strangers (sending 60%), a reciprocity norm emphasizing fairness and generosity, and a reasoning frame grounded in kindness, honesty, and positive social expectations.

### Dictator Game  
The participant sends $3 of $5 (60%) to the other person, keeping $2. The other person has no power to reject. Participant’s thoughts: "kindness," "generousity," "karma," "friendly," "helpful," "handsome."  

Compared to the Ultimatum Game sender offer (100% sent), the Dictator offer is lower by 40%. This difference suggests that the participant’s higher Ultimatum offer is partly strategic, motivated by the recipient’s power to reject. The Dictator giving reflects moderate pure altruism or fairness motivation, but less than the strategic giving in the Ultimatum Game.
