The stated Values scale (A.3.3) is moderately reliable relative to behavioral game data. The participant’s Social Desirability score is not provided here, but the values ratings show a balanced pattern without strong acquiescence compression. However, there is a notable stated-vs.-revealed gap: the participant rates Altruism and Compassion highly (6-8), yet in the Trust Game sender role, they send nothing, indicating distrust and selfishness. Downstream simulation should weigh behavioral data more heavily for trust-related decisions but consider stated values for fairness and social preferences.
