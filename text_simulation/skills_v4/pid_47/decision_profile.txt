## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 150% (estimated from switching points). The participant switches from sooner-smaller to later-larger rewards at relatively high thresholds, indicating impatience but not extreme. They prefer immediate rewards when the delay is short or the difference small but are willing to wait for larger delayed rewards when the difference is substantial. This rate is above typical US adult averages (~20%-200%), placing them in the more impatient half of the population.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: +0.3 (positive, moderate present bias). The participant shows a moderate present bias, preferring immediate rewards more than predicted by their base discount rate. This is directionally consistent with their tightwad spending mode, reflecting cautiousness and some impulsivity in immediate reward preference.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a tightwad spending style, characterized by difficulty spending money and a strong preference for frugality and restraint. They have a moderate maximization tendency, searching somewhat exhaustively for good options but not obsessively. Their need for cognitive closure is moderate to low, indicating tolerance for ambiguity and a deliberative decision style. A strong promotion focus drives them toward ideals, growth, and gain-framed choices, making them responsive to opportunities and advancement. Their conscientiousness is extremely high, supporting organized and systematic decision execution. Metacognitively, they show mild overconfidence and overplacement relative to slightly below-average cognitive ability, suggesting some miscalibration but not severe. Their time preferences reveal moderate impatience and present bias, consistent with their spending restraint. The most predictive feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their strong promotion focus, which will shape their responsiveness to gain versus loss framing and opportunity-oriented decisions.
