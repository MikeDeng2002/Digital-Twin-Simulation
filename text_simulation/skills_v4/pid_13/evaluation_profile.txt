### Ultimatum Game  
Sender: Offered 40% of $5 to the other person (kept $2, sent $3).  
Receiver: Rejects offers of $5, $4, $1, and $0 kept by sender (i.e., rejects offers where self gets $0 or $1 or $4 or $5), accepts offers where sender keeps $2 or $3 (self gets $3 or $2). Minimum acceptable offer is $2 to self (i.e., rejects offers giving less than $2).  

This participant’s fairness norm is moderately generous as a sender, offering a fair 60% split to the other. As a receiver, they enforce a fairness floor by rejecting very low offers (less than 40% to self). There is symmetry between sender generosity and receiver intolerance for unfairness, indicating a fairness motivation rather than pure self-interest.

### Trust Game  
Sender: Sent 60% of $5 to the other person (kept $2, sent $3).  
Receiver returns for each received amount:  
- Received $15: returns $8 (53%)  
- Received $12: returns $6 (50%)  
- Received $9: returns $5 (56%)  
- Received $6: returns $3 (50%)  
- Received $3: returns $1 (33%)  

The participant applies an approximately proportional return rule, generally returning about half of the received amount, except for the smallest amount where they return one-third.  

Participant's thoughts (sender): "I wanted to be fair," "I wanted as close to an even split," "I didn't want to be viewed as crooked," "I wanted to be viewed as honest."  
Participant's thoughts (receiver): "I wanted to split the money as evenly as possible," "I wanted to be thought of as fair," "I wanted to be thought of as an equal."  

This indicates moderate trust in anonymous strangers (sending a majority of the endowment), and a reciprocity norm emphasizing fairness and equality. The thought listings reveal a fairness and reputation frame guiding decisions.

### Dictator Game  
Sent 40% of $5 (kept $3, sent $2). The other person had no power to reject.  
Participant's thoughts: "I wanted to be fair," "I didn't want to be viewed as a cheapskate or cheater," "I wanted to make sure the other person was happy," "I wish there'd been an even split of $2.50 apiece," "I would rather someone else be happier than myself," "I am selfless instead of selfish."  

The Dictator offer is lower than the Ultimatum offer (40% vs. 60%), indicating some strategic giving in the Ultimatum Game to avoid rejection. The Dictator giving still shows a meaningful fairness motivation, but less generosity when strategic incentives are absent.
