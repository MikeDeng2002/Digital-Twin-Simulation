This orientation implies greater responsiveness to gain-framed and opportunity-framed choices, with less sensitivity to loss-framing or safety-framing. This score is consistent with their moderate present bias and spendthrift tendencies, suggesting openness to opportunities and some willingness to invest or spend for gains.

## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 200% or higher. The participant consistently prefers the sooner-smaller reward over the later-larger reward across all three multiple price lists, never switching to the delayed option. This extreme impatience indicates a very high discount rate, far above typical US adult ranges (20%-200%). They always prefer immediate rewards, showing strong impatience in intertemporal trade-offs.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: Approximately +0.5 (positive). The participant shows a moderate present bias, preferring immediate rewards more than the base discount rate predicts. This is directionally consistent with their spendthrift score, reflecting a tendency to favor immediate gratification and some difficulty delaying consumption.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a moderately spendthrift spending mode, occasionally struggling to limit spending but generally mindful and thoughtful about possessions. They are a strong maximizer, searching exhaustively for the best options, and have a moderate need for cognitive closure, balancing preference for order with tolerance for ambiguity. Their regulatory focus is strongly promotion-oriented, making them more responsive to gain-framed and opportunity-framed choices. They are highly conscientious, organized, and efficient in decision execution, with well-calibrated metacognition showing neither overconfidence nor social comparison bias. However, they display extreme impatience in time preferences, always favoring immediate rewards and showing moderate present bias. The most predictive feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their extreme impatience as reflected in the discount rate, which will strongly influence their intertemporal decisions and trade-offs under uncertainty.
