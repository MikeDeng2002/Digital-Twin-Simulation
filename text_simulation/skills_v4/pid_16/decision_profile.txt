## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 200% or higher. The participant consistently prefers the sooner-smaller reward over the later-larger reward across all three multiple price lists, never switching to the delayed option until the delayed reward is substantially larger. This pattern indicates extreme impatience, with a very high discount rate well above typical US adult ranges (20%-200%). They strongly prefer immediate rewards over delayed ones.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: Positive and moderate. The participant shows present bias, preferring immediate rewards more than the base discount rate predicts, but not extremely so. This present bias is directionally consistent with their moderate Tightwad-Spendthrift score, reflecting some caution in spending and a preference for immediacy.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a balanced spending style, neither tightwad nor spendthrift, and a satisficing decision search approach. Their moderate need for cognitive closure and balanced regulatory focus suggest they are neither strongly driven by gains nor losses, responding moderately to framing effects. They are fairly conscientious and well-organized in decision execution. Metacognitively, they are well calibrated with no overconfidence or social comparison bias. However, they display extreme impatience in time preferences, strongly favoring immediate rewards, accompanied by moderate present bias. The most predictive feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their extremely high discount rate, which will strongly influence their intertemporal choices and time trade-offs.
