## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 400% (very high). The participant consistently prefers the sooner-smaller reward over the later-larger reward across all three multiple price lists, never switching to the later option. This extreme impatience indicates a very high discount rate, far above typical US adult ranges (20%-200%). The participant is highly impatient when trading off sooner versus later rewards, showing a strong preference for immediate gratification.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: Approximately +0.9 (strongly present-biased). The participant shows a strong present bias, preferring immediate rewards much more than the base discount rate predicts. This is directionally consistent with the moderate spendthrift score, reflecting a tendency to favor immediate consumption and less self-control over delayed rewards.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a moderately spendthrift consumption mode, with a satisficing decision style and a low need for cognitive closure, indicating tolerance for ambiguity and a flexible approach to decision-making. Their regulatory focus is slightly prevention-oriented, making them more sensitive to loss and safety framing in choices. They are conscientious and efficient in decision execution. Metacognitively, they are mildly underconfident and believe they perform slightly worse than peers, despite above-average cognitive ability, indicating good calibration. Their time preferences reveal extreme impatience and strong present bias, favoring immediate rewards heavily over delayed ones. The most predictive single feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their extremely high discount rate and strong present bias, which will strongly influence intertemporal trade-offs and impulsivity in decisions.
