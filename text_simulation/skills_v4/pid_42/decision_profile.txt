## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 150%. The participant consistently prefers the sooner-smaller reward over the later-larger reward across all three price lists until the later reward becomes substantially larger, indicating a relatively high discount rate and impatience compared to typical US adults (20%-200%). This suggests a moderately impatient time preference, valuing immediate rewards more strongly than delayed ones.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: +0.35 (positive, indicating present bias). The participant shows a moderate present bias, preferring immediate rewards more than predicted by their base discount rate. This present bias is directionally consistent with their moderate tightwad-spendthrift score, reflecting some caution in spending but also a tendency to favor immediacy in reward.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a balanced spending style, neither overly tight nor spendthrift, with a moderate tendency to maximize decisions and a moderately high need for cognitive closure favoring decisiveness and structure. Their regulatory focus is strongly promotion-oriented, making them more responsive to gain and opportunity framing in choices. They are highly conscientious, organized, and efficient in decision execution. Metacognitively, they are slightly overconfident but well calibrated given their high cognitive ability. Their time preferences reveal moderate impatience and present bias, valuing sooner rewards but not extremely so. The most predictive feature for simulating this person’s choice behavior is their strong promotion focus, which will shape their responsiveness to gain-framed versus loss-framed decision contexts.
