## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 150% (estimated from switching points). The participant switches from preferring sooner-smaller to later-larger rewards only when the delayed reward is substantially larger, indicating a relatively high discount rate and impatience compared to typical US adults (20%-200%). ### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: Approximately +0.4 (positive). The participant exhibits moderate present bias, preferring immediate rewards more than the base discount rate predicts. This is consistent with their tightwad spending mode, reflecting cautiousness and a preference for immediate certainty over delayed gains.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a cautious and somewhat tightwad spending style, reluctant to spend freely but able to limit spending effectively. They show a moderate maximization tendency, searching for good options but not exhaustively. Their need for cognitive closure is moderately high, favoring quick decisions and definite answers, which aligns with a moderately promotion-focused regulatory style oriented toward gains and aspirations. Conscientiousness is moderate to low, consistent with self-reported disorganization and a casual approach to punctuality. Metacognitively, they are mildly overconfident but reasonably calibrated given their cognitive ability. Their time preferences reveal impatience and moderate present bias, preferring immediate rewards unless delayed rewards are substantially larger. The most predictive feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their moderately high need for cognitive closure combined with a promotion focus, which will shape their responsiveness to gain-framed opportunities and decisiveness under uncertainty.
