## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 400% (derived from consistent preference for sooner-smaller rewards over later-larger rewards across all three price lists, with switching only at the highest delayed amounts). The participant always preferred the sooner-smaller option until the delayed reward was substantially larger, indicating a highly impatient discount rate. This rate is well above typical US adult ranges (20%-200%), showing strong impatience in intertemporal trade-offs.

This participant is very impatient when trading off sooner versus later rewards, heavily discounting future gains and favoring immediate gratification.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: +0.5 (positive). The participant shows a moderate present bias, preferring immediate rewards more than the base discount rate predicts. This present bias is directionally consistent with their slightly tightwad spending mode, reflecting a tendency to prefer immediate control over resources despite some caution in spending.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a moderately tightwad spending style with some inconsistency but generally cautious money management. They tend to maximize in decision search, seeking the best options rather than settling quickly, and have a high need for cognitive closure, favoring quick, decisive choices. Their regulatory focus is strongly promotion-oriented, making them more responsive to gain-framed opportunities and aspirational goals. They are fairly conscientious and organized in decision execution. Metacognitively, they are moderately overconfident but paired with above-average cognitive ability, indicating mild miscalibration without serious risk. Their time preferences reveal strong impatience and present bias, favoring immediate rewards substantially over future ones. The most predictive feature for simulating this person’s choice behavior is their high discount rate and present bias, which will strongly influence intertemporal decisions and impulsivity.
