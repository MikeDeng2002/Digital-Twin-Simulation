The participant consistently prefers the sooner-smaller reward in nearly all choices, switching only when the later-larger reward is substantially better. ### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: Approximately +0.8 (strongly present-biased). The participant shows a strong preference for immediate rewards beyond what the base discount rate predicts, indicating impulsivity or a strong present bias. This is directionally consistent with the moderately tightwad spending mode, as present bias often correlates with difficulty delaying gratification, though the participant’s spending mode is more controlled, suggesting some internal tension between impatience and spending restraint.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a highly conscientious and promotion-focused decision style, oriented toward achieving ideals and gains and responding strongly to opportunity framing. They have a strong need for cognitive closure, favoring quick, decisive action and structured environments. Their decision search style leans toward maximization, seeking the best options rather than settling early. Metacognitively, they are underconfident and underestimate their relative cognitive ability, which may temper their decisiveness despite their high closure need. Time preference measures reveal extreme impatience and strong present bias, indicating a tendency to heavily discount future rewards. Their spending mode is moderately tightwad, showing controlled and mindful spending despite impatience in temporal trade-offs. The most predictive single feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their high Regulatory Focus score, which will strongly influence their sensitivity to gain-framed versus loss-framed decisions.
