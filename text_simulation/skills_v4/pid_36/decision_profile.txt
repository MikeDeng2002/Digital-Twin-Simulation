## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Extremely high (participant always preferred the sooner-smaller reward over the later-larger reward in all three price lists, never switching). This pattern indicates extreme impatience and a strong preference for immediate rewards over delayed ones, far exceeding typical US adult discount rates (20%-200%). The participant consistently chooses immediate payoffs, suggesting a steep discounting of future rewards.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: Strongly positive. The participant shows a pronounced present bias, preferring immediate rewards much more than predicted by their base discount rate. This is directionally consistent with their moderate Tightwad-Spendthrift score, which shows neither extreme tightwad nor spendthrift tendencies but a controlled spending style that may coexist with impatience for immediate gratification.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a highly conscientious and promotion-focused decision style, characterized by thoroughness, systematic planning, and a strong orientation toward gains and ideals. They have a low need for cognitive closure, indicating comfort with ambiguity and a preference for open-ended, flexible decision processes. Their metacognitive calibration is excellent, with accurate self-assessment and realistic social comparison. However, they display extreme impatience and strong present bias in time preferences, consistently favoring immediate rewards over delayed ones. Their spending mode is balanced and controlled, neither tightwad nor spendthrift, aligning with their conscientiousness and metacognitive accuracy. The most predictive single feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their extreme impatience and present bias in discounting future rewards, which will strongly influence intertemporal trade-offs and impulsivity in decision-making.
