## Risk and Loss Evaluation

### Risk Aversion  
The participant consistently chooses the lottery option over certain smaller amounts until the certain amount approaches the full lottery expected value, indicating very high risk aversion in the gain domain. For example, in multiple gain lotteries (50% chance of $6, $8, or $10), the participant prefers the lottery over certain amounts up to nearly the full expected value, switching only at the highest certain amounts. This pattern shows strong risk aversion, as the participant prefers the gamble rather than smaller but more certain payoffs only when the certain payoff is close to or exceeds the expected value.

### Loss Aversion  
In loss lotteries, the participant consistently prefers the sure loss over the gamble involving a larger possible loss, indicating strong loss aversion. For mixed gambles (50% chance of losing $8 and 50% chance of winning various amounts), the participant always prefers the sure $0 over the gamble even when the potential gain is as high as $20, showing extreme loss aversion. The lambda coefficient is therefore well above 1.0, indicating losses are weighted much more heavily than equivalent gains. The combination of very high risk aversion and very high loss aversion is a consistent pattern, reflecting strong avoidance of uncertainty and losses.
