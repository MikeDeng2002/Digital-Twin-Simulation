## Summary  
This participant exhibits a moderately spendthrift spending mode with occasional difficulty limiting spending, paired with a strong maximization decision style and high conscientiousness, indicating thorough and systematic decision execution. Their regulatory focus is promotion-oriented, making them more responsive to gain-framed and opportunity-based choices. However, they show a large overconfidence and overplacement bias relative to their actual cognitive ability, suggesting a risk of miscalibrated confidence in their decisions. Their time preferences reveal very high impatience and strong present bias, consistent with their spending tendencies. The most predictive feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their high discount rate combined with strong overconfidence, which will influence impulsivity and risk assessment under uncertainty.
