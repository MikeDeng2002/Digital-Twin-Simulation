## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 200% or higher. The participant consistently prefers the sooner-smaller reward over the later-larger reward across all three multiple price lists, never switching to the later option until the amounts are equal or better. This indicates extreme impatience, with a very high discount rate well above typical US adult ranges (20%-200%). They strongly prefer immediate rewards over delayed ones.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: Approximately +0.8 (strongly positive). The participant shows a strong present bias, preferring immediate rewards much more than predicted by their base discount rate. This is directionally consistent with their moderately spendthrift spending mode, as present bias often correlates with difficulty delaying gratification and controlling spending impulses.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a decision-making profile characterized by strong present bias and extreme impatience in intertemporal choices, favoring immediate rewards heavily over delayed ones. They have a high need for cognitive closure, preferring quick, definite decisions and showing discomfort with ambiguity. Their regulatory focus is moderately prevention-oriented, making them somewhat more sensitive to loss and safety framing. They tend to satisfice rather than maximize, settling for good enough options without exhaustive search. Spending behavior is moderately spendthrift, with occasional trouble limiting spending but less trouble spending when needed. Metacognitively, they are slightly underconfident and modestly underestimate their performance relative to peers, consistent with their below-average cognitive ability. The most predictive single feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their extreme impatience and strong present bias, which will heavily influence time trade-offs and impulsive decisions.
