### Trust Game  
Sender: Sent 100% of $5 to the other person (sent all $5).  
Receiver returns:  
- Received $15: returned $8 (53%)  
- Received $12: returned $6 (50%)  
- Received $9: returned $5 (56%)  
- Received $6: returned $3 (50%)  
- Received $3: returned $1 (33%)  
The participant applies a roughly proportional return rule, returning about half of the amount received, except for the smallest amount where the return is exactly one-third.  
Participant's thoughts (sender): "I want to maximize the amount of money received for both of us. I should send all money. I hope this person understands what I'm doing. I'm not going to feel stupid if this person pockets all the money. I really want this money to be tripled and I get sent half. I wish I could communicate with this person."  
Participant's thoughts (receiver): "I want to send half back. This person is sending me less. I'm going to keep sending less and less if this person sends less. I wouldn't have had a problem being fair if this person wasn't like this. At this point I might as well keep it all. I'm keeping all I'm sent."  
This participant shows very high trust as sender, sending all money to maximize joint payoff. As receiver, they apply a reciprocity norm of returning about half, but their thoughts reveal conditional reciprocity and some frustration with perceived unfairness, leading to reduced returns if the sender sends less. The reasoning frame is fairness and reciprocity, with strategic adjustment based on the other’s behavior.
