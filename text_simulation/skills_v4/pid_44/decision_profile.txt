## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 400% (extremely high impatience). The participant consistently prefers the sooner-smaller reward over the later-larger reward across all three multiple price lists, never switching to the later option. This indicates extreme impatience and a strong preference for immediate rewards over delayed ones, far exceeding typical US adult discount rates (20%-200%). Such a high discount rate suggests difficulty delaying gratification and a strong present-oriented decision style.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: Approximately +0.9 (strong present bias). The participant shows a strong present bias, preferring immediate rewards much more than predicted by their base discount rate. This is directionally consistent with their moderate Tightwad-Spendthrift score, as their moderate spending mode does not contradict a strong preference for immediacy in time trade-offs.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a highly conscientious and promotion-focused decision style, characterized by a strong drive toward ideals, creativity, and achievement, and a preference for gain-framed opportunities. They search exhaustively for the best options, reflecting a maximization tendency, and maintain moderate tolerance for ambiguity, leading to balanced decisiveness. Metacognitively, they are underconfident and modest in social comparison, aligning with slightly below-average cognitive ability, indicating realistic self-assessment. Their time preferences reveal extreme impatience and strong present bias, consistently favoring immediate rewards over delayed ones. Spending behavior is moderate and controlled, neither tightwad nor spendthrift. The most predictive single feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their extremely high discount rate and strong present bias, which will strongly influence their intertemporal choices and impulsivity under uncertainty.
