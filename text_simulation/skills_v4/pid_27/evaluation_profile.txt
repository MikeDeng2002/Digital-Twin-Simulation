## Risk and Loss Evaluation

### Risk Aversion  
The participant consistently chooses the certain option over lotteries in gain-domain multiple price lists until the certain amount exceeds the lottery expected value by a substantial margin, indicating strong risk aversion. For example, in the $6 lottery (50% chance), the participant prefers the sure amount up to $5.75 and only switches to the lottery at $7.00, showing a high certainty premium. This pattern is consistent across lotteries with increasing stakes. The participant never switches to the lottery at low sure amounts, indicating a strong preference for certainty over risky gains.

### Loss Aversion  
In loss-domain lotteries, the participant prefers sure losses over gambles with equal or slightly better expected values, indicating strong loss aversion. For example, in a 50% chance to lose $6 vs. sure loss options, the participant prefers sure losses close to the maximum loss, switching only at relatively small sure losses. The lambda coefficient is high, indicating losses are weighted substantially more than equivalent gains. Cross-referencing with risk aversion, both risk and loss aversion are strongly aligned, showing a consistent pattern of cautious, prevention-focused evaluation of uncertain outcomes.
