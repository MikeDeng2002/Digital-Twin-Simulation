## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 200% or higher. The participant consistently prefers the sooner-smaller reward over the later-larger reward across all three multiple price lists, never switching to the delayed option until the delayed reward is equal or better. This indicates extreme impatience and a strong preference for immediate rewards over future gains. Compared to typical US adult discount rates ranging from 20% to 200%, this participant is at the high end or beyond, showing very impatient time preferences.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: Strongly positive (present-biased). The participant shows a strong present bias, preferring immediate rewards much more than the base discount rate predicts. This is directionally consistent with the moderately tightwad spending mode, reflecting a tendency to prefer immediate gratification despite cautious spending.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a moderately tightwad spending style, carefully controlling expenditures but not to an extreme degree. They tend to satisfice rather than maximize, showing a moderate need for cognitive closure that favors decisiveness and order. Their regulatory focus is slightly prevention-oriented, making them more sensitive to loss and safety framing in decisions. Conscientiousness is fairly high, supporting organized and efficient decision execution. Metacognitively, they are well calibrated with no overconfidence or social comparison bias, consistent with above-average cognitive ability. However, their time preferences reveal extreme impatience and strong present bias, indicating a strong preference for immediate rewards despite their cautious spending. The most predictive feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their extreme impatience as reflected in the high discount rate and present bias, which will strongly influence intertemporal trade-offs and impulsivity under uncertainty.
