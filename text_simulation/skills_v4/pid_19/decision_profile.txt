## Spending and Consumption Mode

Score: 18 (scale: 4 = extreme tightwad, 26 = extreme spendthrift, midpoint ~13). This participant exhibits a moderately spendthrift default spending mode. Their self-rating slider is relatively high (8 on a 1-11 scale, closer to spendthrift), and they report sometimes having trouble limiting spending as well as sometimes having trouble spending money, indicating some ambivalence but a general tendency toward spending rather than tight control. The mall scenario response aligns with this moderate spendthrift tendency, as they identify more with the person who spends rather than the one who restrains spending. The four items show reasonable internal consistency, all pointing toward a moderate spendthrift profile rather than tightwad.

## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 400% (participant consistently prefers sooner-smaller rewards over later-larger rewards across all three price lists until the very last options). The participant always chose the sooner-smaller option in the first nine items of each list, switching only at the very end to the later-larger option, indicating extreme impatience. This is a very high discount rate, well above typical US adult ranges (20%-200%), showing strong preference for immediate rewards over delayed ones.

This extreme impatience suggests the participant heavily discounts future rewards and prefers immediate gratification.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: +0.85 (strongly present-biased). The participant shows a strong present bias, preferring immediate rewards much more than predicted by their base discount rate. This is directionally consistent with their moderate spendthrift score, as present bias often correlates with less self-control in spending and a tendency to favor immediate consumption.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a moderately spendthrift consumption mode, tending to spend rather than restrict, with some ambivalence about spending control. They are a strong maximizer, searching exhaustively for the best options, and have a moderate to somewhat high need for cognitive closure, favoring decisiveness and clear answers. Their regulatory focus is strongly promotion-oriented, making them more responsive to gain-framed and opportunity-based choices. They are fairly conscientious and organized in decision execution. Metacognitively, they are underconfident relative to their above-average cognitive ability, indicating good calibration and modest self-doubt. Their time preferences reveal extreme impatience and strong present bias, heavily favoring immediate rewards over delayed ones. The most predictive single feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their extreme impatience and strong present bias, which will strongly influence their intertemporal choices and impulsivity in spending decisions.
