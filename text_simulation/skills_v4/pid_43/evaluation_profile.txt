## Risk and Loss Evaluation

### Risk Aversion  
CRRA coefficient: Approximately 0.5 (positive, indicating risk aversion). The participant consistently prefers certain smaller amounts over risky lotteries with equal or higher expected values in the gain domain, switching only when the certain amount approaches the lottery expected value. For example, they prefer $6 in 6 weeks over $3 in 5 weeks, $8 in 7 weeks over $4 in 6 weeks, and $10 in 7 weeks over $5 in 5 weeks until the certain amount increases sufficiently. This pattern shows moderate risk aversion in gains, favoring sure smaller gains over risky larger gains until the sure gain is close to or exceeds the expected lottery value.

### Loss Aversion  
Lambda coefficient: Approximately 2.0 or higher (losses weighted about twice as much as gains). The participant consistently prefers to avoid losses, choosing sure smaller losses over risky larger losses, and rejects mixed gambles unless the gain is substantially higher than the loss. For example, they prefer sure losses of about half the expected loss in lotteries and reject mixed gambles with gains up to $20 against $8 losses. This indicates strong loss aversion. Cross-referencing with risk aversion, both are aligned in the same direction: moderate risk aversion and strong loss aversion, a consistent pattern indicating cautious evaluation of uncertain outcomes, especially when losses are possible.

### Ultimatum Game  
Sender: Offered 20% of $5 to the other person (kept $4, sent $1).  
Receiver: Accepted offers of $5, $4, $3, and $2 (i.e., when the sender keeps $0, $1, $2, or $3), but rejected offers of $1 and $0 (sender keeps $4 or $5). Minimum acceptable offer is $2 (40% of the total).  
This participant’s fairness norm as a sender is relatively selfish, offering only 20% to the other person, which is unusually low compared to typical offers of 40-50%. As a receiver, they enforce a meaningful fairness floor by rejecting offers below 40%, indicating intolerance for very low offers. This asymmetry suggests strategic giving: the participant offers little but expects a minimum fairness threshold from others.
