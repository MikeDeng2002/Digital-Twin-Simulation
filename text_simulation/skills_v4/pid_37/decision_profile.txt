## Summary  
This participant exhibits a balanced spending mode, neither tightwad nor spendthrift, with consistent self-control in spending behavior. They are a strong maximizer, searching exhaustively for the best options, and have a moderate need for cognitive closure, allowing for some tolerance of ambiguity without rushing decisions. Their regulatory focus is slightly promotion-oriented, making them more responsive to gain-framed choices and opportunities. They are highly conscientious and systematic in decision execution. Metacognitively, they are strongly overconfident and overplace themselves relative to peers, but this is justified by their perfect cognitive test performance, indicating well-calibrated high ability. Their time preferences reveal extreme impatience and strong present bias, consistently preferring immediate smaller rewards over larger delayed ones. The most predictive single feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their extreme impatience and present bias, which will strongly influence intertemporal trade-offs and impulsive decision tendencies.
