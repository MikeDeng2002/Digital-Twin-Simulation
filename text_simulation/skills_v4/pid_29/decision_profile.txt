## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate

The participant consistently prefers immediate smaller rewards to delayed larger rewards, switching only when the delayed reward is substantially better. Present bias coefficient: Approximately +0.6 (positive and moderate). The participant shows a clear present bias, preferring immediate rewards more strongly than predicted by their base discount rate. ## Summary  
This participant exhibits a moderately spendthrift consumption style, tending to spend rather than restrain, with some difficulty limiting spending. They show a moderate maximization tendency, searching for good options but not exhaustively. Their very high need for cognitive closure suggests they prefer quick, decisive choices and dislike ambiguity, favoring structured and predictable decision environments. Their regulatory focus is moderately promotion-oriented, indicating responsiveness to gain and opportunity framing, consistent with their altruistic and community-oriented self-description. Metacognitively, they are underconfident despite high cognitive ability, indicating a notable miscalibration that may affect their decision confidence. Their time preferences reveal a highly impatient discount rate and moderate present bias, aligning with their spendthrift tendencies and preference for immediate rewards. The most predictive single feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their very high Need for Cognitive Closure, which will strongly shape their decisiveness and intolerance for ambiguity in uncertain decisions.
