### Ultimatum Game  
Sender: Offered 40% of $5 to the other person (kept $2, sent $3).  
Receiver: Accepted offers when the sender kept $0, $1, $2, or $3 (i.e., offers of $5, $4, $3, $2 to self), but rejected offers when sender kept $4 or $5 (offers of $1 or $0 to self). Minimum acceptable offer is $2 (40%).  
The participant’s fairness norm as sender is moderately fair, offering a near-equal split but keeping slightly more than half. As receiver, they enforce a fairness floor by rejecting very low offers ($1 or $0). This shows a balanced fairness norm: willing to share fairly but not tolerate very unfair offers. There is no asymmetry; sender generosity and receiver tolerance align.

### Trust Game  
Sender: Sent 40% of $5 to the other person (kept $3, sent $2).  
Receiver returns:  
- Received $15 (sent $5 x3): returned $5 (33%)  
- Received $12 (sent $4 x3): returned $6 (50%)  
- Received $9  (sent $3 x3): returned $3 (33%)  
- Received $6  (sent $2 x3): returned $2 (33%)  
- Received $3  (sent $1 x3): returned $1 (33%)  
The participant applies a mostly consistent proportional return rule, generally returning about one-third of the amount received, except for the $12 case where they return half.  
Participant's thoughts (sender): "I would want to keep as much money as possible," "I also want to be fair to the other person," "keeping slightly more than half is still rather fair considering the situation," "I wonder if the other person would be as fair as I am trying to be."  
Participant's thoughts (receiver): "I want to be as fair as possible," "I don't know if the other person would be fair and send back anything," "I think dividing the money as equally as possible would still be the fairest thing that I can do."  
This indicates moderate trust in anonymous strangers (sending 40%), a reciprocity norm favoring fairness and proportional sharing, and a reasoning frame focused on fairness and cautious optimism about reciprocity.

### Dictator Game  
Sent 40% of $5 (kept $3, sent $2). The other person had no power to reject.  
Participant's thoughts: "I need to be as fair as possible," "There is no way to completely evenly split five dollars," "I'll have to split it as evenly as I can," "How much would the other person likely give me in this situation," "I think giving myself the larger of the two small amounts is fair, since I am the one dividing it."  
The Dictator offer matches the Ultimatum offer (both 40%), indicating that giving is driven by fairness preferences rather than strategic considerations. The participant aims for fairness even when the recipient cannot punish, reflecting genuine altruism or fairness motivation.
