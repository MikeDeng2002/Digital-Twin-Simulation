## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 400% (derived from consistent preference for sooner-smaller rewards over later-larger rewards across all three price lists, with no switching point observed; participant always preferred sooner-smaller). This indicates extreme impatience in intertemporal trade-offs, far exceeding typical US adult rates (20%-200%). The participant strongly discounts future rewards, preferring immediate gratification.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: Strongly positive (present-biased). The participant shows a strong present bias, preferring immediate rewards much more than the base discount rate predicts. This is directionally consistent with their moderate Tightwad-Spendthrift score, as moderate spending control coexists with strong impatience for immediate rewards.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a moderately balanced spending style, neither tightwad nor spendthrift, with consistent self-reports of controlled spending behavior. They tend toward maximization, searching thoroughly for the best options, but have a relatively low need for cognitive closure, indicating tolerance for ambiguity and a deliberative decision style. Their regulatory focus is prevention-oriented, making them more sensitive to loss-framed choices and safety concerns rather than gain-framed opportunities. They are highly conscientious and organized in decision execution. Metacognitively, they are strongly overconfident and overplace themselves relative to peers despite average cognitive ability, indicating notable miscalibration. Their time preferences reveal extreme impatience and strong present bias, consistently preferring immediate smaller rewards over larger delayed ones. The most predictive single feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their extreme impatience as reflected in the very high discount rate and strong present bias, which will strongly influence intertemporal decisions and impulsivity under uncertainty.
