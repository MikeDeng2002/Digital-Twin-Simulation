## Time Preferences

### Discount Rate  
Annualized discount rate: Approximately 150%. The participant’s switching behavior in the multiple price lists shows a strong preference for sooner-smaller rewards over later-larger rewards, indicating impatience. They often choose the immediate or earlier option unless the delayed reward is substantially larger. This discount rate is high compared to typical US adult rates (20%-200%), placing them toward the more impatient end of the spectrum.

### Present Bias  
Present bias coefficient: +0.35 (positive). The participant shows a moderate present bias, preferring immediate rewards more than the base discount rate predicts. This present bias is directionally consistent with their spendthrift spending mode, as both reflect a tendency toward immediate gratification and difficulty delaying rewards.

## Summary  
This participant exhibits a spendthrift consumption mode, often struggling to limit spending and inclined toward immediate gratification. They are a strong maximizer, searching exhaustively for the best options, and have a high need for cognitive closure, favoring decisiveness and clear answers. Their regulatory focus is strongly promotion-oriented, making them more responsive to gain-framed and opportunity-based choices. Despite moderate conscientiousness, they show a large overconfidence and overplacement bias, significantly overestimating their cognitive abilities relative to actual performance, indicating a notable metacognitive miscalibration. Their time preferences reveal impatience and moderate present bias, consistent with their spending and decision styles. The most predictive single feature for simulating this person on choice tasks is their strong promotion focus, which shapes their responsiveness to framing and opportunity cues.
